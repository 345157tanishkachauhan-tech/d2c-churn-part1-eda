# Business Memo

## Objective

Identify customer behaviours associated with churn and recommend areas for further investigation.

## Key Findings

### 1. Customer Inactivity is a Major Risk

Customers with higher recency_days values show significantly higher churn rates, indicating that inactivity is a strong predictor of customer loss.

### 2. Lower Purchase Frequency Increases Churn Risk

Customers purchasing less frequently are more likely to churn compared to highly engaged repeat buyers.

### 3. Support Issues Impact Retention

Customers generating higher support-ticket volumes exhibit increased churn behaviour, suggesting dissatisfaction or unresolved issues.

### 4. Low Digital Engagement Signals Future Churn

Customers with fewer sessions_30d and lower campaign_clicks_30d values demonstrate higher churn tendencies.

### 5. Return Behaviour is Associated with Churn

Customers exhibiting elevated return_rate_180d values appear more likely to churn, indicating possible product or expectation mismatches.

## Recommended Business Actions

1. Target inactive customers before they become dormant.
2. Improve support-ticket resolution quality and response time.
3. Launch engagement campaigns for customers with declining activity.
4. Monitor customers exhibiting high return behaviour.
5. Prioritize high-value customers showing early churn signals.

## Conclusion

The analysis suggests that customer inactivity, declining engagement, support dissatisfaction, and return behaviour are key drivers of churn. Retention initiatives should focus on these risk indicators before implementing broad discount-based campaigns.
